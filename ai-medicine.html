Timeline: AI in Medicine

Hover over any dot to see the full story

1959ML Is Born

Machine Learning is Born

Arthur Samuel at IBM creates a checkers-playing program and coins the term "machine learning." The first computer that learned from experience rather than explicit instructions.

1972MYCIN Launched

MYCIN: The First Medical AI

Stanford's MYCIN used 600 rules to diagnose bacterial infections as well as expert physicians. It proved AI could reason like a doctor, even if it never saw clinical use.

1989Neural Nets Spot Cancer

Neural Networks Detect Cancer

Researchers first applied neural networks to medical images, teaching computers to spot abnormalities in X-rays — the foundation of modern radiology AI.

2012Deep Learning Wins

The Deep Learning Revolution

Geoffrey Hinton's team wins ImageNet using deep learning, slashing image recognition errors. Within years the same tech reshaped medical imaging.

2016DeepMind Reads Eyes

DeepMind Reads the Eye

Google DeepMind's AI diagnosed eye disease from retinal scans as accurately as world-leading ophthalmologists — the first clear match to specialist doctors.

2017Stanford Beats Derms

Stanford AI Beats Dermatologists

A Stanford algorithm trained on 130,000 skin images matched board-certified dermatologists at diagnosing skin cancer. Published in Nature.

2020AlphaFold Cracks Proteins

AlphaFold Solves Protein Folding

DeepMind cracked a 50-year-old mystery — predicting 3D protein shapes from amino acid sequences. A revolution for drug discovery.

2022FDA Approves AI Dx

FDA Approves Autonomous AI

The FDA approved the first AI that can diagnose a condition — diabetic retinopathy — entirely on its own, without a doctor reviewing the result.

2023GPT-4 Passes USMLE

ChatGPT Passes the Medical Exam

GPT-4 passed the US Medical Licensing Exam at or above the passing threshold. Physicians began debating AI's role in clinical reasoning.

2024AI-Made Drugs in Trials

AI-Designed Drugs Enter Human Trials

The first drug molecules designed entirely by AI entered Phase 1 human trials, led by Insilico Medicine and Recursion Pharmaceuticals.
