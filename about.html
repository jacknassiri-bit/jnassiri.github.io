About the author

A student writer at the edge of medicine.

I'm a rising junior at Loyola High School in Los Angeles, where I write for the school newspaper, The Loyalist. I started The Med Frontier because I wanted a place to write about the topics I care most about, on my own timeline, without waiting for the next issue.

I'm fascinated by the collision between artificial intelligence and medicine. We're living through a moment where algorithms are reading X-rays, predicting drug interactions, and assisting in surgery, and most people don't even know it's happening yet. That gap between what's actually occurring in research and what the public understands is exactly what I want to help close.

My goal is simple: take complex developments in medical AI and emerging technology and make them readable for anyone who's curious. If a story is worth covering, it's worth explaining clearly.

What I focus on

AI in medicine: how machine learning, large language models, and computer vision are reshaping diagnosis, drug discovery, and clinical decision-making. And new medical technology: robotic surgery, smart implants, wearable biosensors, and next-generation imaging tools that are quietly transforming patient care.

I try to always ask: what does this actually mean for patients? Technology means nothing without the human on the other end of it.

Note: I'm a high school student, not a medical professional. Nothing on this blog is medical advice. I aim for accuracy and always link to primary sources, but please consult a doctor for anything health-related.

Why "The Med Frontier"?

A frontier is a place where the known world ends and the unknown begins. That's exactly where medicine is right now, especially with AI. This blog is my attempt to report from that edge, as clearly and honestly as I can.
