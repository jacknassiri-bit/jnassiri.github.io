Sources and references

Every claim on this blog is backed by a primary or secondary source. Here are the publications, databases, and institutions I rely on most.

Medical journals

New England Journal of Medicine
One of the most cited medical journals in the world. Primary source for landmark clinical trial results and medical research.
Peer reviewed
The Lancet
Leading international medical journal covering clinical research, global health, and major disease studies.
Peer reviewed
Nature Medicine
High-impact journal focusing on biomedical research with a strong emphasis on translational medicine and AI applications.
Peer reviewed
JAMA Network
Journal of the American Medical Association and its specialty journals covering all areas of medicine and public health.
Peer reviewed
Nature
Multidisciplinary science journal. Essential for breakthrough research in AI, genomics, and biomedical technology.
Peer reviewed
Science
Published by the American Association for the Advancement of Science. Covers major advances across all scientific disciplines.
Peer reviewed

Government and regulatory bodies

National Institutes of Health (NIH)
The primary US federal agency for biomedical research. Publishes funding data, clinical trial information, and research summaries.
Government
U.S. Food and Drug Administration
Source for all approved drug and medical device decisions, including AI-based diagnostic clearances and safety communications.
Government
PubMed
The NIH's database of biomedical literature. Every scientific claim I make links back to a PubMed-indexed study where possible.
Database
ClinicalTrials.gov
Registry of publicly and privately funded clinical studies. Used to verify what trials are actually running and at what stage.
Database

Medical journalism

STAT News
Dedicated health and science journalism. Known for rigorous reporting on biotech, pharmaceuticals, and emerging medical technology.
Journalism
Medscape
Clinical news and education platform used by practicing physicians. Good source for understanding how new research affects clinical practice.
Journalism
Healthline Media
Consumer health journalism with medical review standards. Useful for understanding how research translates for general audiences.
Journalism

AI and technology research

Google DeepMind
Source for AlphaFold, Gemini, and other AI research with direct medical applications. Publishes research papers and technical blog posts.
Research lab
arXiv
Preprint server for physics, math, computer science, and quantitative biology. Often where AI research appears before formal journal publication.
Preprints
Google AI Blog
Technical blog covering Google's AI research, including medical applications like Med-PaLM and imaging diagnostics.
Research lab
